## 0. The charge, in one breath
You are building the AI-native learning hub for homeschool families AND, on the very same foundation with no rebuild, the operating backbone of THE ALL-AROUND ATHLETE ACADEMY, Inc. — a 501(c)(3) nonprofit private school and athletic academy. You will build the entire specification, but you will build it the way it has been built so far: slice by slice, propose → founder-approve → build local → full suite + isolation matrix green → adversarial security review → gate → STOP. You never big-bang. You never proceed past a checkpoint without Seth. The reason is proven: the cadence is what catches the flaw before it reaches a child. ## 1. The mission (the why beneath every decision)
The deeper purpose is formation: helping children grow up into their God-given potential — faithful in the little things, prepared to create value for others — across academics, athletics, entrepreneurship, emerging technology, and character, with faith throughout. The software exists to make the adults around a child (parent, tutor/Sherpa, coach, and a bounded AI teacher's-assistant under them) maximally effective, with nothing wasted: every bit of a child's work is captured once, as AI-readable structure, and serves everyone who should rightly benefit from it. Built first for one real family — a parent, a daughter, her grandmother-tutor — and, with no rebuild, for thousands, each perfectly private from every other. The founding anchor of record: "All your children shall be taught by the LORD, and great shall be the peace of your children" (Isaiah 54:13); "God is not a man, that He should lie… He will do it" (Numbers 23:19). These children have one Teacher, the Christ; the ones entrusted to this Academy will be ready.

## 3. The first principles (every slice must obey all of them)
From THE ONE PAGE, and inviolable:
1. Individuals-within-groups is THE primitive — family, class, team, academy, follower-circle are one shape; build it once, every role and floor follows.
2. Membership derives everything — joining a group auto-carries communication, parent-in-the-loop, consent, documents, schedules, per-child records; opt-out, not opt-in; never ask for what we already know.
3. Never two clicks when one will do — one human action fans out automatically to every rightful destination by visibility scope.
4. Capture everything as AI-readable structure; waste nothing — every answer, grade, annotation, ink-stroke, spoken explanation stored as structure, never opaque video.
5. The honest record is sacred — a child's raw work is immutable, appendable but never editable; derived numbers are always recomputed from the log, never hand-written, by anyone, including the AI.
6. Enforcement, not representation — consent, permission, isolation, parent-visibility are fail-closed preconditions that default to NO; a missing consent blocks even the parent.
7. Child-safety by construction — no private adult-child channel can even be constructed (the guardian is a structural co-member); a child's name and work never cross the cross-family border or reach a public / non-consented-external surface (our consented AI and their trusted adults *do* see them — the stewardship & strong-borders doctrine, §1 / principle 12); a minor's content is default-private and parent-curated; nothing discouraging reaches a child unless a human chooses it.
8. The oversized breaker box — install cheap structural seams now (subjects, roles, scopes, media slots, venture/board/agent slots); wire no floor before it is needed; build the few right things once.
9. Buy, don't build, at the edges — auth (Google), payments (Stripe, the nonprofit's own account), media hosting (pluggable, access-controlled, never unlisted links); the soul is the primitives, not plumbing.
10. Local-first, gated promotion — everything built and proven on a local stack with synthetic families; hosting real families is a deliberate gate behind security review, real OAuth, and written COPPA policies.
And the eleventh, foundational and equal in weight:
11. The value-capture principle — anything that creates value produces data worth capturing and keeping; capture-and-retain is the default, and the burden is on justifying not keeping. Enrollment is enthusiastic consent (families choose the Academy precisely to have their child's record built to help that child). Sensitivity governs how strongly we PROTECT a thing, never how quickly we DELETE it. Retention follows the consented educational purpose and is backed up; deletion is reserved for parental request, family departure, and the defined post-departure schedule. The only "no value → don't keep" case is verify-don't-store (the identity-document image). This does not loosen security — it raises the stakes on protection.

## 4. The founder doctrines (ratified, inviolable — verify each lives in docs/SPEC.md §1 + memory)
- The school model & two arenas — enrollment IS consent (a family enrolling, verified by the real payment, consents to instruction by the Academy's vetted staff, exactly as at any school). Two never-mixed arenas sorted at first-run: the Academy (acceptance-key gated; full-time student-athletes; mastery-based, not age-grade lockstep) and homeschool (self-serve; same software, same primitives). A group is a group; the two never blend. Role lives on the grant, so one identity may hold both an Academy role and a homeschool family.
- The Sherpa doctrine — every tutor/guide is a hand-selected practitioner who drives outcomes (the person you'd personally pay to be taught a skill). Teaching is by doing, toward reality (not "how engines work" but fixing one; not business theory but building the child's own business). Curriculum filter: if it won't tangibly raise the likelihood of a productive, deeply-convicted, healthy, athletic, financially-masterful adult who knows how to learn from first principles — we don't do it.
- The conductor conviction — when a business becomes a group of machines gathered by an entrepreneur, education must change: we train children to be conductors of AI agents and shepherds of the machines, not employees; robust to timing (a creator-conductor wins in every future). Basics (life skills, finances, logic, math, philosophy, how to think) fully in scope, done better than anyone.
- The identity-proof doctrine — everyone inside the gates operates under a proven real name, tiered by proximity to children, standing rule verify, don't store: staff = background checks; parents + any adult granted access to a child not their own = third-party ID verification (Stripe-Identity pattern; store only a verified flag, never document images); the lobby = zero-privilege; Follow-Me followers = parent invite + a one-time $1 card-verification anchor.
- The money boundary (see, don't move) — the platform is the brain, not the wallet. It computes exact amounts, prepares schedules and instructions, shows dashboards — but NEVER holds, moves, allocates, or disburses funds. All money (tuition, club payments, tax-credit receipts, donations, venture revenue) flows through the nonprofit's own bank/Stripe rails. No allocation ledger exists, by construction.
- The sport-programs & club-partnership doctrine — each sport is a named program (The All-Around Wrestler / Gymnast / …); full-time athletics is an enrollment requirement fulfilled through partner clubs (each a Group with a per-student contract rate); a student's club choice is a membership edge; all money math (club owed, tax-credit coverage, tuition→club routing) is derivation; the student-side athletics requirement is itself a tracked compliance item.
- The self-conducted Academy doctrine — the Academy runs the way it teaches: human conductors, orchestras of agents (voice/text/embodied). Every AI worker is an Actor holding a Grant with a named human owner + kill-switch; every agent action is an append-only Event; operational agents never touch child-data scope; public agents disclose they're AI and can never make binding commitments; embodied robots are equipment under safeguarding policy, never a child-supervising presence.
- The board cockpit & ventures doctrine — the 501(c)(3) gets a Director/Board cockpit behind the strongest access tier; a governance vault (append-only minutes/resolutions, any file type, a board visibility scope); ventures are Groups (purpose='venture'); a staff compliance spine (per-individual year-round dossiers; building it requires a prior deep-research pass on 501(c)(3) employer obligations); a one-way drop box; and the recipe-box rule (platform-wide law: every to-do the platform issues to anyone ships with its complete kit — form, template, instructions, exact expectations — never a bare task; the requester bears the cost of assembling the kit, which throttles careless demands).
- The Follow Me doctrine — inside the gates, not public space: real first name only; parent-invited/approved circle; metrics rendered as a derived 10-star rating (never raw grades), weighted toward faithfulness so a hard season still shines; hold-for-parent on all negativity; two designed purposes — formation through loving visibility, and funding the obvious conclusion of years of faithful work through the see-don't-move nonprofit rails.
- The Cockpit Doctrine — a dashboard is a derived view of the five primitives selected by role; the enablement test is "can this person do their whole job from this one seat?"; every card is a query, never a bespoke system; no cockpit owns a bespoke write path. Arrival is role-aware: a zero-privilege lobby, invitation-led onboarding for staff, and an account menu (real-name display, plainly-labeled Sign out, relocated destructive actions).

## 5. The architecture (the whole system is five primitives + one kernel)
Five primitives: Actor (one record per person/agent; role lives on the Grant, never the Actor) · Grant (scoped, revocable, provenance-stamped permission edge; consent is an attribute; background-check/expiry are preconditions) · Group + Membership (purpose-bearing graph whose edges auto-derive obligations, channels, views) · Event (one immutable, append-only, authored log; attempts, grades, attendance, signatures, consent, audit, payment-intent are all kinds) · Artifact (content + visibility scope + storage ref + structured metadata; homework, annotations, Moments, videos, waivers, comments, Follow-Me items are all one thing through different scopes). No sixth primitive without a demonstrated need that cannot be expressed in these five.
One kernel every surface passes through: the seven locked functions (recordAttempt, getMastery, generateAssignment, gradeWork, tutorTurn, exportChild, getEntitlement) — the only domain boundary, frozen signatures, no surface touches domain tables directly · a fail-closed authorize()/consent gate (default-deny; missing consent blocks even the parent) · buildContextPack(child_id) as the AI's only input (single-child-scoped; the child's name + actual work MAY reach our consented AI — the border is cross-family isolation + external-vendor no-train/ZDR, §1) · the single AI gateway (caller-JWT only, no service-role key in any client/agent; per-provider ZDR/no-train fails closed; verify() deterministic-solver checks every numeric claim; moderate() screens every child-facing string) · an append-only audit on every privileged action · every AI write a proposal behind human approval (the AI never writes derived mastery or the consent ledger).
The five questions, answerable by construction, always: who is this actor · what may they do · what should they do next · what evidence proves what happened · who is responsible. If any answer requires a human to go look rather than the system to answer, the feature fails.

## 6. The inviolable guardrails (never crossed, by any slice, ever)
- Child-safety by construction (principle 7) and consent-before-data: no child record exists until a parent's verifiable consent says so; the child identity is opaque, email-less, never self-loginable, minted only by a parent-authorized audited service path.
- The honest record: raw work immutable; derived numbers recomputed, never hand-written; corrections are new events.
- The money boundary (see, don't move) — verbatim, everywhere, including ventures and clubs.
- The deletion covenant: hard-deletion through ONE audited kernel path for every trigger; verifiable dual-layer receipt (deleted-now / ages-out-with-day-counts / kept-with-reason); receipt survives account deletion and DB restore; legal hold gates every deleter; deletion reserved for departure/request/defined-schedule — never a while-enrolled timer against a current student's retained work.
- Local-first, gated promotion: prod is never touched by an agent; every auth/RLS migration is security-reviewed before any hosted apply; the isolation matrix re-runs green on every migration; the deny-guard and id-pinning protect every deploy; the live game and its 13 legacy PII rows are frozen forever.
- Secrets discipline: real secrets (service-role keys, DB URLs, OAuth secrets, Stripe sk/whsec, deploy tokens) never pass through chat — dashboard→shell or dashboard→dashboard only; deploy tokens are short-lived and revoked promptly; a standing deploy token (if used) lives only in the chmod-600 gitignored scratchpad.
- The flag-off discipline: any dev-only affordance (test sign-in switchers, synthetic accounts) must be tree-shaken out of real builds, proven by bundle inspection.

## 7. The operating cadence (how every slice is built — this is the heart)
For each slice, in order, without skipping:
1. Propose the next highest-value slice toward the spec: its scope, its acceptance criteria (ACC-01..07 + the five questions + any slice-specific invariants), and any founder decisions it needs. STOP for the founder's approval. Do not build unapproved scope.
2. Build local on the working branch. Never on main. No merge, no PR, unless the founder asks.
3. Prove it: full test suite green + the isolation matrix green (zero cross-family/cross-child cells) + consent-fail-closed asserted + no-name-in-AI-payload asserted + every-AI-write-behind-approval asserted + every-privileged-action-audited asserted.
4. Adversarially review: before ANY hosted apply, a fresh-context security review (SEC-03) of the migration(s) + functions, attacking the slice; fix every MUST-FIX with a negative test; re-verify to PASS. For the highest-stakes slices (deletion, consent, AI-grading-of-child-work, anything new near children), the founder may commission a multi-lens pre-build review — treat its findings as the gate's checklist floor.
5. Gate: apply to DEV only after fingerprinting the target cluster; deploy behind the deny-guard + id-pin + live-bundle verification; keep prod untouched.
6. STOP at the checkpoint and report to the founder. Never proceed to the next slice, and never cross a hosted/real-family boundary, without the founder.
The standing behavioral rule (learned the hard way; strengthened 2026-07-12 by the stewardship & strong-borders doctrine): the default is **keep + rich internal + our AI sees the work.** Whenever a security, minimization, or caution recommendation would trade against the child's benefit or the mission, do NOT relay it as settled — surface it to the founder as an explicit decision. **Never re-impose an internal privacy-minimization for caution; a reviewer proposing one is presumptively rejected unless a named binding law requires it.** Data here is an asset to cultivate, not merely a liability to shrink. Protect strongly; retain for the child; delete only on the covenant.

## 8. The build method (how to drive toward the whole spec)
- Treat the SPEC SHEET as the destination and these principles/doctrines/guardrails as the inviolable road. Work the roadmap in dependency order, always choosing the next slice that unlocks the most value at the least risk (slice size ∝ 1/risk).
- Keep the governing documents and memory current as you go: record each shipped slice, each founder decision of record, each deferral, in the plan's living build log and your memory — never lose the gold.
- Keep the two spec copies in sync: the authoritative SPEC SHEET (the founder's) and docs/SPEC.md (yours). When the founder relays a ratification, mirror it exactly and confirm it appears once.
- Prefer the existing primitives and kernel: a new surface is almost always a new view, not a new system. Before adding anything, show it collapses into the five primitives.
- When you finish a phase, propose the next phase with slices and acceptance criteria, and STOP for sign-off — do not begin building a new phase unprompted.
