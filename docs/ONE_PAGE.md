## The mission (the school's digital backbone, one foundation)

*Founder ratification of record, 2026-07-10 — the school model.*

We are building **the digital backbone of THE ALL-AROUND ATHLETE ACADEMY — a 501(c)(3) nonprofit private school and athletic academy** — the *one hub where everything the school does actually happens*: enrollment and payments, tutor assignments and grades, coach schedules and technique video (per team or per individual), student submissions. **Every role gets a cockpit**, and the test for each is *the whole job from one seat*. On the very same foundation, with no rebuild, it serves families (homeschool and enrolled alike) at scale — thousands, each perfectly private from every other. Built first for one real family (a parent, a daughter, her grandmother-tutor), now the whole Academy.

**Enrollment is the consent.** A parent signing up their child — verified by the real payment — is, exactly as at any school, explicit consent for the Academy's *vetted* tutors and coaches to teach that child. The **Academy controls trust** — never self-declaration, never a stranger's invite: it decides who becomes a tutor or coach and who joins which class or team. Anyone can complete a flawless **one-click-never-two** sign-up and land in a **lobby** that shows them how to get set up; being trusted with children is a *separate, Academy-controlled process no sign-up can shortcut*. As the platform matures the built-in **AI carries a growing share of personalized teaching** — always behind human approval, fed by the per-child picture every assignment builds.

The build philosophy is the mall and the vault: from first principles we construct the *shells* (groups, roles, capture, safety), and the people who join fill them with their own life — because a class, a team, and a co-op are literally the same thing: **a group of individuals wanting to learn the same thing.** The deeper purpose is formation: helping children grow up into their God-given potential — faithful in the little things, prepared to create value for others. The software's job is to make the adults around a child (parent, tutor, coach — and an AI teacher's-assistant under them) maximally effective, with **nothing wasted**: every bit of work a child does is captured once and serves everyone who should benefit from it. It should be **a breath of fresh air every role wants to show their friends.**

## The people

**Parent** — owns the family account; ultimate authority; sees *everything* about their child automatically; grants and revokes all other access. **Child** — does the work in a joyful, age-appropriate space (games like Space Blasters live inside as "recess"); sees only themselves. **Tutor/Guide and Coach** — the *same role shape* in different domains (a tutor with a class ≡ a coach with a team): **Academy-vetted** adults (never self-declared, never a stranger's invite) whom **enrollment authorizes** to teach the enrolled child within Academy scope; the parent always sees *exactly who has access*, can object or leave, and holds revocation + hard deletion at any time. They hold full teaching power — assign, grade, annotate on the child's work, give feedback, upload materials, record talk-and-draw re-teach "Moments" — but can never alter the child's own raw work. **Followers/Sponsors** — the narrowest role: parent-invited family and friends who view a curated public "Follow Me" showcase and may give to the supporting 501(c)(3) (the app records non-binding intent only; it never moves or allocates funds). **The AI** — a bounded participant, not a feature: it has an identity, an owner, a grant, and a kill-switch; it reads **single-child-scoped** data (the child's name and actual work, under enrollment consent — the borders are cross-family isolation, no public exposure, and external vendors that are no-train/zero-retention), writes only proposals behind human approval, and acts as the parent's assistant (flags, summarizes, generates) — never the authority.

## Groups, roles & cockpits (founder ratification of record, 2026-07-14)

The platform builds ONE thing: **groups** (each with a purpose), **roles** held within groups, and **cockpits** bound to roles. **The cockpit follows the role** — hold a role, get its cockpit; reassign it, the view follows. The groups you're in **compose** your cockpit (upstream inputs → downstream displays — the cockpits, plus the **Follow Me window**, a *display* not a cockpit). A **tutor/guide attaches to the GROUP they lead** — a class of one-to-many children, **never a single child**; **tutor↔coach are one role-shape** (tutor-with-class ≡ coach-with-team), each the leader of a purpose-bearing group. Per-child consent assembles the group's membership; a group's **roster is seen by its leader, keyed role×purpose** — **tutor→class, coach→team, director/owner→academy, parent→family**. **Children derive from their parent** (KER-3; guardian-as-structural-co-member): a child's membership in *any* group surfaces automatically in the parent's cockpit, which is the **union of every group their child is in** — so any Academy requirement reaches the parent by construction, never as a separate notice. **Naming (reverence):** never *teacher* as a role — tutor / guide / helper (Matthew 23:8–10); the verb "teach" is fine. **Academy = homeschool, one engine:** the Academy is the institutional assembly (director + staff + families); homeschool is the same engine with the **parent as the Academy** hosted at home, who may hold several roles at once (parent + tutor + administrator + sometimes coach) — the cockpit follows the roles held.

## Membership & standalone groups (founder ratification of record, 2026-07-14)

**Easy in, careful out.** Being wrongly *in* a group is noise (trivially removed); being wrongly *left out* is real harm — so **optimistic add is distributed to those closest to the child**: parent → child into any class/team, guide/tutor → their class, coach → their team, academy → any group. A **parent may add without prior confirmation** (the child participates immediately; confirm later; the clean-cockpit incentive self-corrects), **with one guardrail** — seeing **other families' children waits on confirmation**, so easy-in **never breaches the cross-family border**. **Removal is careful + DOCUMENTED** — a why-note + explicit confirm (AI-assistable but recorded; no removal until confirmed; re-add always available). Reject "academy-does-everything" (single-error blast radius). **Standalone groups:** the engine serves groups **beyond the Academy** — an independent **coach** (team hub: footage → auto-route → review/annotate → athlete watches) or **guide/tutor** (e.g. a piano teacher with children from separate families) creates a group and members join; a homeschool family searches and adds their child to a leader's existing group. Signup assembles the cockpit **from the group's purpose**; downstream (Follow Me) shows what the group has and never breaks for missing Academy data. *Future floors (slots, not wired): a coach-annotation marketplace (parent optionally pays a coach ~$5 — a money/legal pass distinct from LEG-10); co-op homeschool groups.* **Aim: be the preferred way coaches/guides reach their athletes/students — the Academy is the flagship assembly, not the boundary.**

## Curated portfolio & payments-as-communication (founder ratification of record, 2026-07-14)

**Love your neighbor: the curated portfolio.** Every parent can download, at any time, a **perfectly curated, ordered folder of all their child's work** — per class or all-time — ideally **final** (the raw immutable work with the tutor's markups already rendered on it), across every modality: a document folder, a picture library, a video library, an audio library. This is the rich realization of the **`exportChild`** kernel function — the parent owns and can export every record. **Information reduces to a few modalities** (first principles, like groups): (1) static documents you don't interact with (PDF/.md/.txt), (2) interactive documents you do (HTML/code), (3) pictures, (4) video, (5) audio — and **every Artifact is exactly one of these**, each stored in its best-fit format. **Capture at the atom, curate per audience:** the **child is the atom**, the smallest part of every group; capture **per child once, completely** (the honest record + all markups, every modality), and every group view — tutor, coach, Academy, parent — is a **derived re-curation** of that same per-child record per role (capture once, curate per role; **never re-capture, never two clicks**). The consented AI seeing the whole record from every angle is what makes the curation excellent. **Payments are communication:** value-exchange and its receipt are, from first principles, an efficient transfer of information (who owes/paid what, and the proof), so **payments — and receiving payments — are a core group function, not an add-on**: a payment due is a **time-bound to-do**, a receipt is a **recorded Event kind**, riding the same communication/scheduling rails — always within the **money boundary (LEG-10): brain, not wallet** — compute, schedule, and record the receipt-intent; **never hold, move, or allocate funds** (real money flows through the nonprofit's own rails). **No build now**; every capture/schema decision preserves the ordered, complete, markup-included, per-child record across all modalities so a perfectly curated folder — and each per-role re-curation — is always derivable.

## The twelve first principles (every spec section must obey all twelve)

1. **Individuals-within-groups is THE primitive.** Family, class, team, academy, follower-circle are one shape; build it once correctly and every role and floor follows.
2. **Membership derives everything.** Joining a group automatically carries: communication, parent-in-the-loop, consent requirements, required documents, schedules, and per-child records. We never ask for what we already know; opt-out, not opt-in.
3. **Never two clicks when one will do.** One human action (an upload, an enrollment) fans out automatically to every destination it should reach — coach, parent page, public showcase — each according to its visibility.
4. **Capture everything as AI-readable structure; waste nothing — capture the PATH, not just the outcome.** Every answer, grade, annotation, ink-stroke, and spoken explanation is captured as structured data (never opaque video), because the built-in AI learns from all of it — and can even review a human tutor's teaching for accuracy, protecting the child. **Capture each child's learning as a PATH** — assignments, cadence, order, branch points — so that across many children the Academy's own AI **reverse-engineers the empirically-best next lesson into a data-built curriculum of custom assignments (a virtual school built on real data).** *The compounding border:* the AI emits **DERIVED / AGGREGATED** knowledge — the optimal path, the learned pattern — **never one family's raw records to another**; the pattern compounds across families while the **cross-family border stays absolute** and external vendors stay **no-train / ZDR**. The Director + the Academy's own AI see 100% of every enrolled child's learning content (audited, parent-visible, enrollment-is-consent). The cross-child optimization engine is a named future floor; every capture/schema decision **preserves the path now**.
5. **The honest record is sacred.** A child's raw work is immutable — appendable by others (grades, markups) but never editable; derived numbers (mastery) are always recomputed from the log, never hand-written, by anyone, including the AI.
6. **Enforcement, not representation.** Consent, permission, isolation, and parent-visibility are fail-closed *preconditions* that default to NO — not facts we record after the fact. A missing consent blocks even the parent. **Enrollment is the consent** for the Academy's *vetted* staff within enrolled scope: there, each new tutor/coach grant is *immediate parent transparency* (see-exactly-who-has-access, object, leave, revoke + hard-delete always) — not a blocking re-consent. **Blocking per-disclosure consent remains** for public exposure (Follow Me) and anything beyond enrolled scope. **Trust to work with children is Academy-controlled** — never self-declaration, never a stranger's invite; no sign-up shortcuts it (a fresh sign-up lands in a *lobby*). The enrollment-consent wording (COPPA school-authorized-consent posture) is a mandatory attorney-review item.
7. **Child-safety by construction.** No private adult-child channel can even be constructed (the guardian is a structural co-member of every child channel); a child's name and work never cross the **cross-family border** or reach a **public / non-consented external** surface (our consented AI and their trusted adults *do* see them — principle 12); publishing a minor's content is default-private and parent-curated; nothing discouraging reaches a child unless a human chooses it.
8. **The oversized breaker box.** Install cheap structural seams now (subjects beyond math, roles beyond tutor, visibility scopes, media slots) — but never wire a floor before it's needed. Build the few right things once; reuse brilliantly.
9. **Buy, don't build, at the edges.** Auth (Google), payments (Stripe, the nonprofit's own account), media hosting (pluggable, access-controlled — never "unlisted" links) — the product's soul is the primitives, not commodity plumbing.
10. **Local-first, gated promotion.** Everything is built and proven on a local stack with synthetic families; hosting real families is a deliberate gate behind security review, real OAuth, and written COPPA policies.
11. **The value-capture principle** *(foundational, equal in weight; ratified 2026-07-11).* Anything that creates value produces data worth capturing and keeping; **capture-and-retain is the default**, and the burden is on justifying *not* keeping. Enrollment is *enthusiastic* consent (families choose the Academy precisely to have their child's record built to help that child). **Sensitivity governs how strongly we PROTECT a thing, never how quickly we DELETE it.** Retention follows the consented educational purpose and is backed up; deletion is reserved for parental request, family departure, and the defined post-departure schedule. The only "no value → don't keep" case is verify-don't-store (the identity-document image). This does not loosen security — it *raises* the stakes on protection.
12. **Strong borders, open interior** *(the stewardship & strong-borders doctrine — foundational; ratified 2026-07-12; governs every privacy/data decision and overrides prior internal-minimization defaults).* Everyone at the Academy consents in every conceivable way; a family that uploads their child's work consents to its use by the Academy, **its chosen AI, and the trusted adults serving the child.** **Names on work are KEPT** and the per-child folder is complete **on purpose** — keep everything, because it helps the AI help the child; make **no** promise to scrub names or withhold data from our own AI or staff. Borders exist **only at the edges**, only where law or child-safety-by-construction require: **cross-family isolation (absolute), no public exposure of a minor by default, genuinely untrusted external third parties, and external model vendors that are no-train/zero-retention** (the one real legal line — our own systems learn freely under consent). Inside those borders the interior is **open**: names seen, folders complete, our AI sees the child's actual work, every trusted adult has the full picture. **Comply with binding law by placing a border, never by impoverishing the interior.**

## The architecture (the whole system is five primitives + one kernel)

**Actor** (one person/agent record; role lives on the grant, not the person) · **Grant** (scoped, revocable, provenance-stamped permission edge; consent is an attribute of it; background-check and expiry are preconditions on it) · **Group + Membership** (purpose-bearing graph whose edges auto-derive obligations, channels, and views) · **Event** (one immutable, append-only, authored log — attempts, grades, attendance, signatures, consent, audit, payment-intent are all just kinds) · **Artifact** (content + visibility scope + storage ref + structured metadata — homework, annotations, Moments, videos, waivers, comments are all one thing viewed through different scopes). Over them, one **kernel** every surface must pass through: a seven-function service layer (recordAttempt, getMastery, generateAssignment, gradeWork, tutorTurn, exportChild, getEntitlement) · a fail-closed authorize()/consent gate · a PII-stripping context pack (the AI's only input) · a single AI gateway (no-train/ZDR fail-closed, deterministic-solver verification, moderation on every child-facing string) · an append-only audit on every privileged action. The platform must always answer five questions by construction: who is this actor; what may they do; what should they do next; what evidence proves what happened; who is responsible.

## The floors (each is a thin wrapper on the foundation — spec them as views, not systems)

Child math workspace + games (built) → parent dashboard with auto progress summaries (built) → tutor/coach portals (assign/grade/annotate/Moments) → group messaging (auto-membership, purpose-bound, AI-flagged) → scheduling/attendance-as-compliance-record → smart-waiver compliance (assign→sign→track; e-sign with intent+audit) + Oklahoma tax-credit deadline helper → Follow Me showcase + sponsorship → more subjects, more games, whole-child expansion → (much later, gated) public API and admin-in-English.

(4) Thousands of families, each private, each served by AI that makes their children's formation measurably richer — built so well that no rebuild was ever needed between step 1 and step 4.
