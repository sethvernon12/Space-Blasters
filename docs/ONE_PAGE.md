## The mission (a dual mandate, one foundation)

We are building an **AI-native learning hub for homeschool families** AND, with the very same foundation, **the operating backbone of THE ALL-AROUND ATHLETE ACADEMY — a 501(c)(3) nonprofit academy**. One trusted place where a student does *all* of their learning and where the Academy runs *all* of its operations: academics, athletics, entrepreneurship, emerging technology, and character. Built first for one real family (a parent, a daughter, her grandmother-tutor), now expanding beyond math to the entire Academy, and — without any rebuild — to thousands of families, each perfectly private from every other. The build philosophy is the mall and the vault: from first principles we construct the *shells* (groups, roles, capture, safety), and the people who join fill them with their own life — because a class, a team, and a co-op are literally the same thing: **a group of individuals wanting to learn the same thing.** The deeper purpose is formation: helping children grow up into their God-given potential — faithful in the little things, prepared to create value for others. The software's job is to make the adults around a child (parent, tutor, coach — and an AI teacher's-assistant under them) maximally effective, with **nothing wasted**: every bit of work a child does is captured once and serves everyone who should benefit from it.

## The people

**Parent** — owns the family account; ultimate authority; sees *everything* about their child automatically; grants and revokes all other access. **Child** — does the work in a joyful, age-appropriate space (games like Space Blasters live inside as "recess"); sees only themselves. **Tutor/Guide and Coach** — the *same role shape* in different domains (a tutor with a class ≡ a coach with a team): trusted adults the parent grants per-child, revocable access; they hold full teaching power — assign, grade, annotate on the child's work, give feedback, upload materials, record talk-and-draw re-teach "Moments" — but can never alter the child's own raw work. **Followers/Sponsors** — the narrowest role: parent-invited family and friends who view a curated public "Follow Me" showcase and may give to the supporting 501(c)(3) (the app records non-binding intent only; it never moves or allocates funds). **The AI** — a bounded participant, not a feature: it has an identity, an owner, a grant, and a kill-switch; it reads only name-stripped data, writes only proposals behind human approval, and acts as the parent's assistant (flags, summarizes, generates) — never the authority.

## The ten first principles (every spec section must obey all ten)

1. **Individuals-within-groups is THE primitive.** Family, class, team, academy, follower-circle are one shape; build it once correctly and every role and floor follows.
2. **Membership derives everything.** Joining a group automatically carries: communication, parent-in-the-loop, consent requirements, required documents, schedules, and per-child records. We never ask for what we already know; opt-out, not opt-in.
3. **Never two clicks when one will do.** One human action (an upload, an enrollment) fans out automatically to every destination it should reach — coach, parent page, public showcase — each according to its visibility.
4. **Capture everything as AI-readable structure; waste nothing.** Every answer, grade, annotation, ink-stroke, and spoken explanation is captured as structured data (never opaque video), because the built-in AI learns from all of it — and can even review a human tutor's teaching for accuracy, protecting the child.
5. **The honest record is sacred.** A child's raw work is immutable — appendable by others (grades, markups) but never editable; derived numbers (mastery) are always recomputed from the log, never hand-written, by anyone, including the AI.
6. **Enforcement, not representation.** Consent, permission, isolation, and parent-visibility are fail-closed *preconditions* that default to NO — not facts we record after the fact. A missing consent blocks even the parent.
7. **Child-safety by construction.** No private adult-child channel can even be constructed (the guardian is a structural co-member of every child channel); a child's name is un-emittable to any AI (never selected); publishing a minor's content is default-private and parent-curated; nothing discouraging reaches a child unless a human chooses it.
8. **The oversized breaker box.** Install cheap structural seams now (subjects beyond math, roles beyond tutor, visibility scopes, media slots) — but never wire a floor before it's needed. Build the few right things once; reuse brilliantly.
9. **Buy, don't build, at the edges.** Auth (Google), payments (Stripe, the nonprofit's own account), media hosting (pluggable, access-controlled — never "unlisted" links) — the product's soul is the primitives, not commodity plumbing.
10. **Local-first, gated promotion.** Everything is built and proven on a local stack with synthetic families; hosting real families is a deliberate gate behind security review, real OAuth, and written COPPA policies.

## The architecture (the whole system is five primitives + one kernel)

**Actor** (one person/agent record; role lives on the grant, not the person) · **Grant** (scoped, revocable, provenance-stamped permission edge; consent is an attribute of it; background-check and expiry are preconditions on it) · **Group + Membership** (purpose-bearing graph whose edges auto-derive obligations, channels, and views) · **Event** (one immutable, append-only, authored log — attempts, grades, attendance, signatures, consent, audit, payment-intent are all just kinds) · **Artifact** (content + visibility scope + storage ref + structured metadata — homework, annotations, Moments, videos, waivers, comments are all one thing viewed through different scopes). Over them, one **kernel** every surface must pass through: a seven-function service layer (recordAttempt, getMastery, generateAssignment, gradeWork, tutorTurn, exportChild, getEntitlement) · a fail-closed authorize()/consent gate · a PII-stripping context pack (the AI's only input) · a single AI gateway (no-train/ZDR fail-closed, deterministic-solver verification, moderation on every child-facing string) · an append-only audit on every privileged action. The platform must always answer five questions by construction: who is this actor; what may they do; what should they do next; what evidence proves what happened; who is responsible.

## The floors (each is a thin wrapper on the foundation — spec them as views, not systems)

Child math workspace + games (built) → parent dashboard with auto progress summaries (built) → tutor/coach portals (assign/grade/annotate/Moments) → group messaging (auto-membership, purpose-bound, AI-flagged) → scheduling/attendance-as-compliance-record → smart-waiver compliance (assign→sign→track; e-sign with intent+audit) + Oklahoma tax-credit deadline helper → Follow Me showcase + sponsorship → more subjects, more games, whole-child expansion → (much later, gated) public API and admin-in-English.

## Success, in order

(1) One child's real learning captured and visibly improving — *done*. (2) One real family — parent, child, grandmother — living in it. (3) The Academy's families onboarded, compliant, communicating. (4) Thousands of families, each private, each served by AI that makes their children's formation measurably richer — built so well that no rebuild was ever needed between step 1 and step 4.
